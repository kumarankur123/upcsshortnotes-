üë¥üèª Population Ageing / Inverted Population Pyramid

  • Definition: Process by which proportion of elderly individuals in population increases over time
  • Reasons: (1) Declining birth rates (2) Increasing life expectancy
  • Example: Japan

ü§∞üèª Pro-natalist Policy

  • Definition: When govt encourages birth rate/fertility rate of an area
  • 2024: Andhra CM Chandrababu Naidu & Tamil Nadu CM M.K. Stalin urged people of their States to have more children
  • Primary Reasons: States with larger population (1) get more seats in Parliament (2) more % funding under tax devolution formula of finance commission

üë´üå©üíÄ Malthusian Theory of Population Growth

British scholar Thomas Robert Malthus (1798):

  • Observation: Food production increases in arithmetic progression while population increases in geometric progression
  • Equilibrium: Nature tries to establish equilibrium between population vs food supply using 'Positive Checks' (‡§∏‡§ï‡§æ‡§∞‡§æ‡§§‡•ç‡§Æ‡§ï ‡§Ö‡§µ‡§∞‡•ã‡§ß) such as famine, epidemics, earthquake
  • Preventative Checks: Humans should initiate to control population growth e.g. family planning (‡§™‡§∞‡§ø‡§µ‡§æ‡§∞ ‡§®‡§ø‡§Ø‡•ã‡§ú‡§®), celibacy (‡§¨‡•ç‡§∞‡§π‡•ç‡§Æ‡§ö‡§∞‡•ç‡§Ø), late marriages

  • Definition: Spacing or preventing birth of children
  • Benefits: Helps limiting population growth and improving women's health
  • Tools: (1) Awareness/Propaganda (2) Free availability of contraceptives (3) Tax disincentives for large families

    • Free drop back from facility to home

üíä Ayushman Bharat: Health & Wellness Centres

  • Services: Preventive, promotive, curative, rehabilitative, palliative care
  • Upgradation: Sub-Centres and Primary Health Centres upgraded to HWCs

üíä Ayushman Bharat: PMJAY (‚Çπ5 Lakh Health Insurance)

  • Coverage: ‚Çπ5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization
  • Beneficiaries: Based on SECC-2011 data. Covers ~10.74 crore poor and vulnerable families (~50 crore beneficiaries)
  • Cashless Treatment: At empaneled public and private hospitals
  • Portability: Can avail treatment anywhere in India
  • Implementation: State Health Agencies (SHAs) at state level
