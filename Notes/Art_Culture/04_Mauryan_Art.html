Ashoka Pillars, Stupas & Edicts (321-185 BC)

üìñ Introduction

The Mauryan Empire (321-185 BC) witnessed the emergence of imperial art and architecture.

  • Emperor Ashoka (269-232 BC) was the greatest patron of art
  • Mauryan art is characterized by monumental pillars, stupas, rock edicts, and palaces
  • Court art (royal patronage) with high technical skill
  • Influenced by Persian Achaemenid art (polished stone, bell-shaped capitals)

üèõÔ∏è Ashoka Pillars Super PYQ

Purpose: To propagate Dhamma (moral & ethical teachings)
Material: Chunar sandstone (from near Varanasi)
Feature: Monolithic (single piece of stone), highly polished surface

üìê Structure of Ashoka Pillar:

Component Description
Shaft Tall, tapering column (40-50 feet high)
Capital Top decorative portion
Ab acus Inverted lotus (Persian influence)
Animal Figure Lion, Bull, Elephant, Horse
Dharma Chakra Wheel on top (now lost in most pillars)

ü¶Å Famous Ashoka Pillars: Super PYQ

  • Sarnath Lion Capital National Emblem of India
    • 4 lions standing back-to-back (originally had Dharma Chakra on top)
    • Abacus shows: Horse, Bull, Elephant, Lion (separated by Dharma Chakras)
    • Below abacus: Inverted lotus
    • Inscription: "Satyameva Jayate" (from Mundaka Upanishad)
    • Found at Sarnath (where Buddha gave his first sermon)
  • Lauriya-Nandangarh Pillar (Bihar)
    • Single lion capital
    • Well-preserved, still standing at original location
  • Rampurva Pillar (Bihar)
    • Bull capital (now in National Museum, Delhi)
  • Sanchi Pillar (MP)
    • Lion capital (4 lions, similar to Sarnath)
üí° Unique Features:
  • Mirror polish (Mauryan polish) - still shines after 2000+ years!
  • No joints - monolithic structure
  • Animal figures are realistic & naturalistic

üìú Ashoka's Edicts PYQ

Types of Edicts:

  • Rock Edicts - Carved on large rock surfaces (14 major rock edicts)
  • Pillar Edicts - Inscribed on pillars (7 pillar edicts)
  • Cave Inscriptions - Found in Barabar caves (Bihar)

Script & Language:

  • Brahmi script (Most edicts, left-to-right)
  • Kharosthi script (Northwest region, right-to-left)
  • Greek & Aramaic (Kandahar, Afghanistan)

Content Themes:

  • Dhamma (moral teachings: non-violence, respect for elders, truth)
  • Conquest of Kalinga & remorse
  • Religious tolerance
  • Social welfare (hospitals, wells, shade trees)
üìç Famous Rock Edict: 13th Rock Edict describes Kalinga War & Ashoka's remorse
