üìñ Introduction

The Indus Valley Civilization (IVC) was the most advanced civilization of the ancient world (contemporary with Egypt & Mesopotamia).

  • Known for urban planning and use of burnt bricks
  • Discovered in 1921 (Harappa) and 1922 (Mohenjo-Daro)
  • Rich artistic traditions: Seals, Sculptures, Pottery, Beads

üîñ IVC Seals Super PYQ

What are Seals? Geometrical shaped artifacts, mainly pictographic in nature

Shapes: Square, Circle, Rectangle

ü¶Ñ Famous Seals:

  • Pashupati Seal (Mohenjo-Daro) Super PYQ
    • Depicts a deity in Yogic posture (cross-legged)
    • Surrounded by animals: Elephant, Tiger, Rhino, Buffalo
    • Two deer at the feet (below the throne)
    • Three-faced deity with horns
    • Considered proto-Shiva (early form of Lord Shiva)
  • Unicorn Seal (Most abundant type)
    • Depicts a one-horned animal (possibly a bull in profile)
  • Peepal Leaf Seal
    • Indicates nature worship

üìù Indus Script on Seals:

  • Written from Right to Left
  • Not yet deciphered (IVC = Proto-history)
  • Average of 5-6 symbols per seal

üè∫ Pottery PYQ

Characteristics:

  • Wheel-made pottery (shows technological advancement)
  • Red/Black painted pottery (most common)
  • Designs: Geometric patterns, animals, plants, birds
  • Shapes: Jars, pots, dishes, vases, perforated jars (for brewing?)
üí° Significance: Pottery shows standardization and mass production capabilities of IVC

üíé Beads, Bangles & Ornaments

üîπ Beads:

  • Chanhudaro: Major Bead Industry center PYQ
  • Materials: Carnelian, Amethyst, Lapis Lazuli (semi-precious stones)
  • Imported Lapis Lazuli from Afghanistan
  • Beads were used in necklaces, headgear

üîπ Bangles:

  • Kalibangan: Major Bangle Industry center PYQ
  • Made from: Terracotta, Shell, Copper, Gold

üîπ Ornaments:

  • IVC people were aware of aesthetic look
  • Used: Necklaces, waistbands, bangles, finger rings, earrings
  • Beauty products: Soap, Lipstick, Kajal, Sindoor (from Cinnabar-Mercury)
