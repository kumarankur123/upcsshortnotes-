The Golden Age (319-550 AD)

üìñ Introduction

The Gupta period (319-550 AD) is called the "Golden Age of India" due to unprecedented achievements in art, literature, science, and culture.

  • Peak of classical Sanskrit literature
  • Development of refined Buddha images
  • Beginning of temple architecture (Nagara style)
  • Excellence in cave paintings (Ajanta)
  • Scientific and mathematical advancements
Major Rulers: Chandragupta I, Samudragupta, Chandragupta II (Vikramaditya), Kumaragupta, Skandagupta

üóø Gupta Sculptures Super PYQ

Characteristics:

  • Material: Red sandstone (Mathura tradition continued)
  • Style: Refined, idealized, spiritual expression
  • Buddha Images: Serene face, half-closed eyes, transparent clothing
  • Expression: Inner peace, spiritual enlightenment

üåü Famous Gupta Buddha Images:

  • Standing Buddha (Sarnath) Super PYQ
    • Most refined Gupta Buddha
    • Transparent clothing (body outline visible)
    • Hands in Dharmachakra Mudra (teaching gesture)
    • Halo with intricate floral designs
    • Below: Deer, Dharma Chakra (First Sermon at Sarnath)
  • Seated Buddha (Mathura)
    • Cross-legged meditation pose
    • Hands in Dhyana Mudra
üí° Innovation: Gupta artists perfected the art of showing transparent clothing on stone, creating an ethereal, spiritual effect.

üõï Early Temple Architecture

Development:

  • Gupta period saw the beginning of temple construction (previously rock-cut)
  • Nagara style temple architecture emerged
  • Temples dedicated to Hindu deities (Vishnu, Shiva, Durga)

Famous Gupta Temples:

  • Dashavatara Temple (Deogarh, UP)
    • Dedicated to Vishnu
    • Shows Vishnu's incarnations
    • Early example of Nagara style
  • Bhitargaon Temple (UP)
    • Brick temple with terracotta decorations
