Iconic Buddha Images (1st-5th Century AD)

üìñ Introduction

During the Kushana period (1st-3rd century AD), the first anthropomorphic (human-form) images of Buddha were created, marking a shift from aniconic to iconic representation.

  • Two major schools emerged: Gandhara (Northwest) and Mathura (North-Central India)
  • Both patronized by Kushana rulers (especially Kanishka)
  • Represented Buddha in human form for the first time
üí° Historical Context: Emperor Kanishka (78 AD) convened the 4th Buddhist Council and accepted Mahayana Buddhism, which encouraged iconic worship.

üèõÔ∏è Gandhara School of Art Northwest India Super PYQ

üåç Location & Period:

  • Region: Northwest India + Afghanistan (present-day Peshawar, Taxila, Swat valley)
  • Period: 1st - 5th century AD
  • Patronage: Kushana rulers (Kanishka)

üé® Characteristics:

  • Greco-Roman Influence Key Point
    • Buddha depicted with Greek features: curly hair, mustache, realistic anatomy
    • Face resembles Apollo (Greek sun god)
    • Wearing heavy robes with deep folds (like Roman toga)
  • Material: Blue-grey schist stone (also stucco, terracotta)
  • Style: Realistic, naturalistic, muscular body
  • Halo: Buddha shown with halo/nimbus around head (borrowed from Greco-Roman sun-worship)
  • Themes: Buddha's life events, Bodhisattvas (especially Maitreya, Avalokiteshvara)

üìç Famous Examples:

  • Fasting Buddha (Lahore Museum) - emaciated Buddha during penance
  • Standing Buddha (multiple examples in museums)
  • Scenes from Buddha's life - Birth, Enlightenment, First Sermon, Mahaparinirvana
üîë Remember: Gandhara = Greco-Roman influence + Schist stone + Heavy robes

üïâÔ∏è Mathura School of Art North India Super PYQ

üåç Location & Period:

  • Region: Mathura (Uttar Pradesh)
  • Period: 1st - 12th century AD (continued into Gupta period)
  • Patronage: Kushana rulers, later Gupta rulers

üé® Characteristics:

  • Indigenous Indian Style Key Point
    • Buddha depicted with Indian features: soft face, broad shoulders
    • Wearing light, translucent clothing (revealing body underneath)
    • Right shoulder usually uncovered
  • Material: Red sandstone (spotted red sandstone of Mathura)
  • Style: Smiling face, spiritual aura, serene expression
  • Standing Posture: Tribhanga (triple-bend posture) - unique to Mathura
  • Themes: Buddha, Jain Tirthankaras, Hindu deities (secular art)

üìç Famous Examples:

  • Headless Sitting Buddha (Katra) - earliest Buddha image from Mathura
  • Standing Buddha - with hands in Abhaya Mudra
  • Seated Buddha - in Dharmachakra Pravartana Mudra
üîë Remember: Mathura = Indian style + Red sandstone + Translucent clothing
