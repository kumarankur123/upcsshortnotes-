3. Rise of Magadha: Why Magadha Won?

  • Strategic Location: Between Ganga & Son river (trade route).
  • Iron Ore: Rich in iron deposits (superior weapons).
  • Elephants: Dense forests = war elephants (military advantage).
  • Fertile Land: Rich agriculture = economic strength.
  • Strong Rulers: Ambitious & capable kings (Bimbisara onwards).

6. Nanda Dynasty (344-321 BC)

  • Founder: Mahapadma Nanda (Shudra origin - revolutionary!).
  • Title: "Ekarat" (Only Sovereign), "Sarvakshatrantaka" (Destroyer of all Kshatriyas).
  • 9 Nanda Kings: Mahapadma + 8 sons (including Dhana Nanda).
  • Military Power: Huge army (mentioned in Greek sources).
  • Hathigumpha Inscription: Mahapadma Nanda dug canal in Kalinga (1st canal mention!).
  • Downfall: Heavy taxation + extortion ‚Üí Public discontent ‚Üí Overthrown by Chandragupta Maurya.
üí° Why Nandas Failed?
1. Over-taxation (even on small items)
2. Autocratic rule
3. No public support

7. Pataliputra: The Imperial Capital

  • Founded by: Udayin (Haryanka).
  • Location: Confluence of Ganga & Son river (modern Patna).
  • Strategic Importance: Trade route hub, fertile land, river transport.
  • Continued as capital for: Shishunaga ‚Üí Nanda ‚Üí Maurya ‚Üí Gupta dynasties.
  • Buddhist Councils: 3rd Council by Ashoka (247 BC), 1st Jain Council here.
  • Greek Account: Megasthenes described its prosperity in "Indica".
