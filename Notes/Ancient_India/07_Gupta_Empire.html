5. Gupta Administration (Decentralized)

  • Not Centralized! Federal system. Kings claimed divine origin.
  • Provinces (Bhukti/Desha): Governed by Uparika.
  • Districts (Vishaya): Governed by Vishayapati.
  • Villages: Governed by Village headman (Gramika).
  • Land Revenue: 1/6th of produce (called "Bhaga" or "Udrang").
  • Forced Labour: "Vishti" - unpaid labor (tax in form of labor).

üí∞ Revenue & Economy

  • Gold Coins: "Dinara" (most famous).
  • Silver Coins: "Rupaka" (first issued by Chandragupta II).
  • Guilds (Shreni): Merchant/artisan organizations. Had judicial powers, fixed wages & prices.
  • Land Grants: Guptas donated maximum villages to Brahmins & temples (Agrahara system).

8. Decline of Guptas

‚öîÔ∏è Huna Invasions (The Main Cause)

  • 1st Invasion: During Skandagupta's reign. Defeated by Skandagupta (Bhitari Inscription).
  • 2nd Invasion (500-530 AD): Led by Toramana, then Mihirakula.
  • Mihirakula defeated by: Yashodharman of Malwa (Aulikara dynasty) - NOT a Gupta!

üìâ Other Causes

  • Weak successors after Skandagupta.
  • Decentralized administration ‚Üí Provinces became independent.
  • Economic decline (costly welfare, land grants reduced revenue).
  • Rise of feudalism (local chiefs became powerful).
