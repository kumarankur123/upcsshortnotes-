🇮🇳 Chapter 9: Indian National Movement

Early Phase (1885-1919): From Moderates to Extremists

🏛️ Foundation of Indian National Congress (1885)

🎯 Background: Super PYQ
  • Growing political awareness among regional organizations
  • 1883: Indian National Conference recognized need for pan-Indian platform
  • Idea formally proposed by Allan Octavian Hume (retired British civil servant)

📅 First Session (December 1885)

  • Place: Gokuldas Tejpal Sanskrit College, Bombay 1885
  • President: Womesh Chunder Bonnerjee (first INC President) President
  • Attendees: 72 delegates
  • Founder (disputed): A.O. Hume

🔍 Safety Valve Theory PYQ

  • Theory: INC created as "safety valve" by A.O. Hume
  • Purpose (British view): Allow grievances to be expressed in controlled manner
  • Prevent: Major uprising against British rule
  • Nationalist View: NOT accepted - INC was natural result of nationalist efforts
  • Reality: Early nationalists used INC as platform for political struggle
  • Outcome: INC transformed into powerful tool against British rule

📚 First Phase (1885-1905): The Moderates

🎯 Aims & Methods

Aspect Details
Main Demand Increase Indian representation in government & political reforms
Focus Area Civil services where Indians faced discrimination
Methods Constitutional agitation, petitions, appeals to British authorities
Approach Avoided direct confrontation with British
Mass Involvement Did NOT involve masses in active struggle
Contribution Laid foundation for future mass movements

👥 Key Moderate Leaders

  • Dadabhai Naoroji: "Grand Old Man of India" - Drain Theory
  • Gopal Krishna Gokhale: Political mentor to Gandhi
  • Pherozeshah Mehta: Bombay leader
  • Mahadev Govind Ranade: Social & political reformer
  • Surendranath Banerjee: Bengal leader

🎯 Achievements

  • Created political awareness among educated Indians
  • Exposed economic exploitation by British
  • Established INC as permanent political organization
  • Built foundation for future mass nationalism

🔥 Second Phase (1905-1919): Rise of Extremists

⚡ Partition of Bengal (1905) Super PYQ

📅 16 October 1905

Division:

  • Eastern Bengal: Muslim-majority (present-day Bangladesh & Assam)
  • Western Bengal: Hindu-majority (Bihar, Bengal, Odisha)

Impact:

  • Sparked nationwide protests
  • Beginning of aggressive phase of freedom struggle
  • Nationalists realized petitions were ineffective
  • Led to rise of extremists who believed in direct action

👊 Key Extremist Leaders (Garam Dal = Hot Faction)

Lala Lajpat Rai Punjab Kesari • Punjab leader

🔥 Lal-Bal-Pal Trio: Famous trio of extremist leaders representing Punjab, Maharashtra, Bengal

🧵 Swadeshi Movement (1905) Super PYQ

📍 Congress Session 1905 (Banaras)

  • President: Gopal Krishna Gokhale
  • Resolution: Beginning of Swadeshi Movement
  • Goal: Self-reliance & economic independence
  • Method: Boycott British goods, promote indigenous industries

🎨 Cultural Impact

  • "Amar Sonar Bangla": By Rabindranath Tagore → later national anthem of Bangladesh
  • "Vande Mataram": From Bankim Chandra's Anandamath → theme song of movement
  • Bharat Mata painting: Symbolizing Mother India became inspirational icon
  • Oriental Society of Art (1907): Established to promote Indian artistic traditions
  • Indian art flourished: Painters moved to Indian themes & temples

👥 Key Contributors

  • Chakravarti Ganesh Deshkar:
    • Proposed Swadeshi in 1904
    • Wrote "Deshkartha" (later banned by British)
  • Chidambaram Pillai:
    • Founded Swadeshi Steam Navigation Company
    • Challenge British shipping dominance
  • Acharya P.C. Ray:
    • Established Bengal Chemical Factory
    • Promoted indigenous industry

❌ Limitations

  • Muslim Support: Struggled to gain Muslim support
  • Reasons:
    1. British "Divide and Rule" policy
    2. Lack of strong Muslim leadership in movement
    3. Movement largely led by Hindu leaders
  • Result: Formation of All India Muslim League (1906)
🕌 Muslim League (1906): PYQ
  • Founders: Nawab Salimullah of Dhaka, Siddharth Khan
  • Support: With British backing
  • Position: Maintained loyalty to British while claiming to represent Muslim interests

⚔️ Moderates vs Extremists

Aspect Moderates Extremists
Leaders Gokhale, Naoroji, Mehta Tilak, Lal Lajpat Rai, Bipin Chandra Pal, Aurobindo
Methods Constitutional methods, selective boycotts Total boycott of British goods & services
Approach Gradual approach Direct action

🤝 Dadabhai Naoroji's Compromise (1906)

Four Key Resolutions: Super PYQ
  1. Rejection of Partition of Bengal
  2. Demand for Swaraj (Self-rule)
  3. Promotion of Swadeshi (Indigenous Goods)
  4. Boycott of British Products & National Educational Institutions

💔 Surat Split (1907) Super PYQ

📍 What Happened

  • Year: 1907 1907
  • President: Rashbehari Ghosh (Moderate)
  • Issue: Differences over reorganization
  • Result: Physical altercation, session ended in chaos
  • Outcome: INC split into Moderates & Extremists

🎭 British "Carrot and Stick" Policy

Carrot (Appeasement):
  • Appeased Moderates with limited reforms
Stick (Repression):
  • Bal Gangadhar Tilak: Imprisoned in Mandalay Jail for 6 years PYQ
  • Lala Lajpat Rai & Bipin Chandra Pal: Jailed for 1 year each

📜 Constitutional Acts (1858-1909)

1. Government of India Act (1858) Super PYQ

  • Formally ended: Company Rule
  • Transferred power: To British Crown
  • Queen's Proclamation (1858): Issued at Darbar Hill, Allahabad
    • Princely states' rights respected
    • No further territorial expansion
    • Religious tolerance promised
    • Indians eligible for administrative services (merit-based)
  • Changes:
    • Governor-General → Viceroy of India
    • Created: Secretary of State for India (British minister)
    • Created: 50-member Council of India
    • Abolished: Board of Control & Court of Directors

2. Indian Councils Act (1861)

  • Introduced limited representation in legislative process
  • Indians could be nominated to legislative councils
  • Decentralization: Restored legislative powers to provinces
  • Portfolio System: Departments headed by specific officers
  • New Legislative Councils: Bengal (1862), Bombay & Madras, Punjab (1897)
  • Viceroy's Power: Issue ordinances during emergencies

3. Indian Councils Act (1892)

  • Increased non-official members to ~20
  • Maintained official majority
  • Expanded functions: discuss budget, ask questions
  • Indirect elections (word "election" not explicitly mentioned)

4. Indian Councils Act (1909) - Morley-Minto Reforms Super PYQ

Key Provisions:
  • Allowed elections to Provincial Legislative Councils
  • Indirect elections through Electoral College (landlords, traders, teachers)
  • Abolished official majority in Provincial Councils (retained in Central)
  • Allowed discussion on budget, voting on separate items
  • Separate electorates for Muslims with lower income qualifications PYQ
  • First Indian to Viceroy's Executive Council: Satyendra Prasad Sinha
❌ Criticism:
  • Failed to satisfy Indian aspirations
  • Separate electorates divided Indian society
  • Reinforced British "Divide and Rule" policy

💣 Revolutionary Nationalism (1876-1915)

🔥 Bengal

Anushilan Samiti (1902):
  • Leaders: Barindra Kumar Ghosh, Jatindranath Mukherjee (Bagha Jatin)
  • Magazine: Yugantar (advocated armed revolution)
  • Method: Bomb attacks & assassinations
Alipore Bomb Case (1908): PYQ
  • Target: Douglas Kingsford (British judge)
  • Attempt by: Khudiram Bose &Prafulla Chaki
  • Mistake: Bombed carriage with 2 British women
  • End: Khudiram hanged, Prafull shot himself
  • Arrested: Barindra Ghosh, Aurobindo Ghosh (acquitted)

🔥 Maharashtra

  • 1897: Chapekar brothers (Damodar & Balkrishna) assassinated W.C. Rand & Lt. Ayerst in Pune
  • 1904: Vinayak Damodar Savarkar formed Abhinav Bharat (secret society)
  • 1909: Assassination of A.M.T. Jackson in Nashik
  • Punishment: Savarkar sentenced to Cellular Jail, Andaman

🌍 Revolutionary Activities Abroad

India House (London, 1905):
  • Founder: Shyamji Krishna Varma
  • Notable: Madan Lal Dhingra assassinated Curzon Wyllie (1909)
  • Madame Bhikaji Cama: Published "Bande Mataram" from Paris
Ghadar Movement (1913-1915): Super PYQ
  • Formed in: USA & Canada by Punjabi expatriates
  • Leaders: Lala Hardayal, Kartar Singh Sarabha, Sohan Singh Bhakna
  • Newspaper: The Ghadar (weekly)
  • Goal: Armed uprising in India (1915)
  • Support: Germany (during WWI)
  • Result: British intelligence crushed, mass arrests & executions
Komagata Maru Incident (1914):
  • 376 Indian passengers denied entry into Canada
  • Forced to return to India
  • British forces opened fire at Budge Budge (Calcutta)
  • Fueled anti-British sentiments

🎯 Other Notable Acts

  • 1912: Ras Bihari Bose & Sachindra Nath Sanyal bombed Lord Hardinge's procession in Delhi
  • Tamil Nadu: Vanchi Iyer assassinated British collector Robert Ashe

🏠 Home Rule Movement (1916-1918) Super PYQ

🎯 Background

  • WWI Hardships: Food shortages, rising prices, economic distress
  • Disillusionment: 1909 Morley-Minto Reforms failed to grant self-rule
  • Political Deadlock: Congress weakened by moderate-extremist divisions
  • Demand: Swaraj (self-rule) gaining momentum

📍 Two Home Rule Leagues

Aspect Tilak's League (April 1916) Annie Besant's League (Sept 1916)
Coverage Maharashtra, Karnataka, Central Provinces Rest of India (Bombay, Madras, UP)
Branches 6 branches 200 branches
Focus Political education, vernacular languages Rapid expansion, attracted major leaders
Notable Leaders - Motilal Nehru, Jawaharlal Nehru, C.R. Das, Lala Lajpat Rai

🔥 British Repression

  • 1917: Annie Besant arrested → mass protests
  • Protest: Subramania Iyer renounced his knighthood
  • Support: Muhammad Ali Jinnah defended Tilak in legal cases

✅ Impact

  • Created nationwide political awakening
  • Strengthened demand for Swaraj
  • Pressured British → Montagu Declaration (1917)
  • Laid foundation for Gandhi's Non-Cooperation Movement (1920)
  • Spread nationalism into rural areas
  • Connected urban elites with masses

🤝 Lucknow Pact (1916) Super PYQ

📍 Key Points

  • Reunited: Congress & Muslim League in joint session
  • Congress Concession: Accepted separate electorates for Muslims
  • Demand: More Indian representation in legislatures
  • Criticism:
    • Set precedent for communal politics
    • Potentially encouraged similar demands from other communities

📜 Montagu Declaration & Reforms (1917-1919)

📅 Montagu Declaration (20 August 1917)

  • British promised responsible government in India
  • Committee formed under Edwin Montagu (Secretary of State for India)

⚖️ Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms (1919) PYQ

Dyarchy in Provinces:
  • Reserved Subjects (British Governor & Executive Council):
    • Law, Finance, Police, Irrigation
  • Transferred Subjects (Indian Ministers):
    • Health, Education, Local Government, Industry
❌ Weaknesses:
  • Governor had overriding powers
  • Real autonomy was weak
  • Failed to satisfy nationalists
  • Led to Non-Cooperation Movement (1920)
